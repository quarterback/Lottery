#11 - Chip Window Engine + Leaderboard Overhaul
What & Why
The Bid Standardization paper has been revised with three fundamental mechanic corrections that change the core chip math, plus a three-tab UI overhaul shown in the attached mockups. The current engine and leaderboard must be rebuilt to match.

Engine changes (paper-accurate)
1 — Pot mechanic (breaks symmetry) The current engine gives the winner their own wager (+bet) and takes the loser's own wager (−bet). The paper says: "The pot is both teams' wagers combined. The winner receives the full pot. The loser loses only their own stake." Net effect: winner gains the opponent's wager, loser loses own wager. This changes chip dynamics entirely — a conservative team (10-chip bid) that beats an aggressive opponent (25-chip bid) gains 25, not 10.

Implementing this requires scheduling chip-window games. For each of the 22 chip-window nights, randomly pair all 30 teams into 15 matchups. Each team's chip gain/loss on a given night depends on what its opponent bid.

2 — Double mechanic (wager×2, not chips×2) The paper says "the double multiplies their wager for that game." The current implementation incorrectly doubles the entire chip total. The correct mechanic: on one home game (requires ≥ 100 chips), a team's wager doubles (25 → 50). The opponent knows in advance and responds with their max bid (25). Pot for that game = 75 (50 + 25). If the doubling team wins, they gain +25 (opponent's bet). If they lose, they lose −50 (own doubled wager). This is a risk asymmetry, not a chip multiplication.

3 — All 30 teams in the chip pool The paper explicitly states: "All 30 teams participate in the chip pool." Safe playoff teams participate because pick-swap rights and owed picks give them direct financial stakes in chip-window games against lottery teams. In the simulation, safe playoff teams bid conservatively (10) by default, or aggressively (25) if they are modeled as having swap exposure. A team labeled "pick swap holder" bids 25 against their exposed lottery team partner to deny them chip gains.

4 — Negative chips allowed "The minimum wager is 10 — there is no zero bid. A team cannot sit out." Teams at or below 0 chips still bid the 10-chip minimum. Chips can go negative. For odds-weight computation, use max(0, chips) — teams below zero receive the record-floor odds but no chip upside.

5 — Draft order = chip ranking Full model: most chips = Pick 1, record irrelevant. The leaderboard must show a FINAL PICK column (1–14) determined by chip standings, not lottery probability. In the "Draft Projection" tab, Pick 1 always goes to the chip leader. The existing odds-floor computation remains for the lottery overlay view (7% floor for all 14 teams).

Engine (chip_window_sim.py)

simulate_chip_window_league builds a 22-night game schedule by randomly pairing all 30 teams into 15 matchups per night. Each matchup stores (home_id, away_id, home_wager, away_wager, pot, winner_id, home_won).
Winner gains opponent_wager chips (net). Loser loses own_wager chips (chips can go negative).
Double mechanic: one home game per season where a lottery team (≥ 100 chips) can double their wager. In that game, the opponent automatically responds with 25. The doubling team gains +25 if it wins, loses −50 if it loses.
All 30 teams have chip trajectories and chip totals in the result JSON. Safe playoff teams are labeled "Safe Playoff" and show their chip total but have no lottery odds.
in_chip_pool is True for all 30 teams.
Each team's result carries: chip_draft_rank (1–14 among lottery teams, by chips desc), chip_pick (same as rank), opponent_wagers (list of 22 opponent wager amounts for trajectory tooltip detail).
Negative-chip teams receive record-floor lottery odds only.

Tasks
Schedule-based chip window — Replace independent per-team simulation with a 22-night pairing loop. Each night randomly pair all 30 teams into 15 matchups; for each matchup, compute each team's wager (based on their strategy), apply pot mechanic (winner gains opp_wager, loser loses own_wager, chips go negative), and record the matchup in a per-night schedule stored in the season result.

Double mechanic rewrite — Remove chips *= 2.0. Implement: lottery team with ≥ 100 chips picks ONE home game per season to declare a double (last eligible home game in the chip window where chips > threshold). For that game, own_wager doubles (25 → 50 or 10 → 20); opponent auto-responds with max (25). Apply standard pot mechanic with the doubled wager. Track doubled flag and double_game_idx.

All-30 chip pool + negative chips — Remove the in_chip_pool = False guard for safe-playoff teams. Give them a conservative strategy by default (MIN_BET); randomly assign ~25% as "pick swap holders" who bid aggressively (MAX_BET) against their lottery-team opponent. Allow chips to go negative. Update _effective_odds to use max(0.0, chips) for weight (negative → floor odds only).

Result JSON shape update — Add to each team's dict: chip_draft_rank (1–14 among lottery teams), chip_pick (same), tonight_opponent (name), tonight_wager (own), tonight_opp_wager, tonight_pot, tonight_double (bool), is_pick_swap_holder (bool for playoff teams), chip_gap_up (chips needed to pass team above), chip_gap_down (chips chasing team needs). Expose a schedule list (22 nights × 15 matchups) on the season JSON for the "Tonight's Games" tab.

Three-tab leaderboard UI — Replace the current two-column layout with three tabs (Chip Standings / Tonight's Games / Draft Projection) driven entirely by client-side JS from the /api/chip-window JSON. Implement the FINAL PICK circles, colored chip bars, TONIGHT column, matchup cards with pot/narrative, and projection table with gap columns, exactly as shown in the three mockup images. Update the explainer text for the pot mechanic and the wager-based double.
